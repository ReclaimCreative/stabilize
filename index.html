Abstract

A Solution for Artists with Essential Tremor

Essential Tremor affects more than 10 million people in the United States. It is characterized by a rhythmic shaking, most commonly in the hands and forearms. For people living with essential tremor, fine motor skills—like those used when drawing—are compromised. For artists, this represents a significant decline in quality of life, since the base function of self expression is undermined.

Reclaim Creative is a proposal to use a combination of robotics and digital signal processing to filter out essential tremor and allow an artist to regain their ability to draw. The system comprises three components: a stylus, a surface outfitted with position sensors, and a Cartesian-style plotting robot capable of illustrating on a 2-D surface.

The stylus is held by the artist inside a sensor zone where time-of-flight sensors triangulate its position. The triangulated signal is filtered to remove tremor content, and the cleaned signal drives the Cartesian plotter to faithfully reproduce the artist's intended motion.

Design Details

System Architecture & Engineering

Cartesian Plotter
Full Plotter Assembly
Five pieces of aluminum V-slot extrusion form the frame. Stationary GT2 belts run the length of each rail, contacting the motor shaft wheels for self-tensioning. Two NEMA 17 steppers drive the Y-axis gantries; one drives the X-axis carriage.
Y-Axis Gantry Plate
Laser-cut gantry plate with four V-wheels engaging the 2040 extrusion rails, providing smooth, low-friction travel along the Y axis.
Pen Lifting Mechanism
Spring-loaded, servo-actuated pen lifter. Neutral state is pen-down; the servo presses upward to lift when no pen-down command is received, protecting the drawing surface during repositioning.
The Stylus
Stylus Cross-Section
Pressing the actuating tip creates a momentary circuit closure via mechanical switch, powering the IR LED at the opposite end. The plotter reads this infrared signal as the pen-down command, enabling precise on/off control without any wireless communication.
Stylus Circuit
Minimal circuit: a 1.5 V AA cell, mechanical switch, 10Ω current-limiting resistor, and infrared LED. No microcontroller required in the stylus itself.
Sensor Array & Position Triangulation
Two VL53L7CX time-of-flight sensors placed a known distance apart on the left side of the active field. The law of cosines yields X/Y Cartesian coordinates from the two measured ranges R1 and R2.
Signal Processing & Control
Signal Processing Pipeline
Raw X and Y position data from the sensor array feeds into an FIR low-pass filter block. The filtered coordinates are then algebraically remapped to discrete stepper-motor steps. Sampling occurs at 60 Hz—well above the Nyquist rate for typical tremor frequencies (5–12 Hz).
